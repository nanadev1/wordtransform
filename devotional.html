His Mercies Are New Every Morning

Lamentations 3:22–23  ·  By Pastor James Boateng

Today's key verse

"The steadfast love of the Lord never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness."
— Lamentations 3:22–23 (ESV)

Every morning you wake up, you are greeted by a fresh supply of God's mercy. Yesterday's mistakes do not carry forward into today's account. Yesterday's failures do not define today's possibilities. God's mercies reset — new, full, and freely given — with every sunrise.

The writer of Lamentations penned these words in the middle of unimaginable grief. Jerusalem had fallen. The temple was destroyed. Yet in the rubble of catastrophe, he found something that could not be destroyed: the faithfulness of God. "Great is your faithfulness," he wrote — not because his circumstances were great, but because God was.

This is the miracle of grace. It is not earned by our performance or lost by our failures. It is rooted in the unchanging character of a God who loves relentlessly. Each morning He says to you: start again. I have not run out. I have not given up. My mercies for you are new.

Whatever you are carrying from yesterday — guilt, regret, disappointment, weariness — you do not have to bring it into today. Receive the mercy that God is extending to you this morning. His faithfulness is the ground beneath your feet.

Reflection questions

1

What from yesterday are you tempted to carry into today that God is asking you to leave behind?

2

How does knowing that God's mercies are new every morning change the way you approach today?

3

In what area of your life do you most need to experience God's faithfulness right now?

Prayer

Heavenly Father, thank You that Your mercies are new this morning. I choose to receive Your grace afresh today. Where I have fallen short, forgive me. Where I am weary, renew me. Where I have lost hope, restore me. Great is Your faithfulness. Let me walk in that truth today. In Jesus' name, Amen.
