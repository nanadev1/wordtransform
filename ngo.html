About WTC Outreach

More Than a Church — A Movement

WTC Outreach is the registered NGO and community development arm of Word Transformation Church. Established in 2013, we exist to translate the values of the Gospel into concrete, life-changing action for families across Accra and the Greater Accra Region.

We operate independently from church giving, partnering with government agencies, international donors, and civic organisations to fund and deliver programmes that are transparent, accountable, and community-led.

  • Registered with the Ghana Department of Social Welfare — fully compliant and accountable
  • Community-led governance — decisions made with the people we serve, not for them
  • Open financial reporting — annual reports published for every stakeholder
  • Faith-inspired, not faith-exclusive — we serve all regardless of belief

What We Do

Our Active Programmes

Six focused initiatives, each designed to address a specific gap in our community with measurable, lasting outcomes.

Education

WTC Scholarship Fund

Providing annual scholarships to bright but financially constrained secondary and tertiary students from low-income homes across Accra. Recipients receive tuition, mentorship, and career guidance.

85 students active Since 2015
Skills

Vocational Skills Hub

Free 3–6 month training in tailoring, hairdressing, electrical work, catering, and digital literacy for unemployed youth and women transitioning out of poverty.

240 graduated Since 2016
Welfare

Family Welfare & Relief

Monthly food packages, utility support, and emergency cash grants for widows, elderly persons without family support, and households in crisis situations.

60+ families Since 2013
Health

Community Health Outreach

Quarterly free medical screening camps, NHIS registration drives, hygiene education, and referral support in partnership with district health services.

600+ screened/yr Since 2018
Women

Women's Economic Empowerment

Micro-loan schemes, business development training, and savings cooperatives for women entrepreneurs — building financial independence from the ground up.

120 women enrolled Since 2020
Environment

Clean Community Initiative

Community clean-up drives, tree planting campaigns, waste management education, and advocacy for a healthier environment in underserved neighbourhoods.

8 communities Since 2022
